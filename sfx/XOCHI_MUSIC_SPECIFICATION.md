# XOCHI - Complete Music Specification
**Aztec Axolotl Warrior Side-Scroller | Xochimilco Setting**

---

## I. CORE MUSICAL IDENTITY

### Sonic Premise
**Xochimilco at twilight + axolotl mystery + side-scroller momentum**

- Avoid tourist-Mexico clichés (sombreros, fiesta loops)
- Honor Xochimilco's mystical ecology (floating gardens, bioluminescence, ancient waterways)
- Balance wonder and danger without grimness
- Create memorable, whistlable melodies that feel both ancient and playful

### Reference Fusion
- **Ori and the Blind Forest**: Emotional water atmosphere, layered ambient textures
- **Guacamelee**: Cultural energy, mariachi-meets-electronic without parody
- **Donkey Kong Country 2**: Layered boss music with clear phase transitions

---

## II. MASTER MOTIF SYSTEM

All tracks must incorporate these core motifs for cohesion:

### 1) Main Xochi Motif (The Axolotl's Call)
**Purpose**: Player identity, homecoming, heroism
**Structure**: 5 notes
**Intervals**: Root → M3 ↑ → P4 ↑ → M2 ↓ → Root
**Example in C minor**: C - Eb - F - Eb - C
**Rhythm**: Quarter, eighth-eighth, quarter, half
**Usage**:
- Must appear in every biome's exploration theme
- Transform for boss battles (darker harmony)
- Brighten for victory (shift to major)
- Fragment for water levels (just first 3 notes)

### 2) Water Motif (Ripple Descent)
**Purpose**: Swimming, canals, peace, fluidity
**Structure**: 3 notes descending
**Intervals**: Root → M2 ↓ → m3 ↓
**Example in C minor**: C - Bb - G
**Timbre**: Always played by soft/liquid instruments (marimba low register, muted bells, pad glides)
**Usage**:
- Background layer in all water sections
- Transition cue between sections
- Healing/checkpoint stinger

### 3) Danger Motif (The Predator's Shadow)
**Purpose**: Threats, bosses, corruption, urgency
**Structure**: 2-note tension
**Intervals**: Tritone or semitone clash
**Example**: F# - C (tritone) or B - C (semitone)
**Timbre**: Distorted bass pulse, metallic percussion, dissonant strings
**Usage**:
- Boss telegraph phases
- Enemy proximity cues
- Dark area ambience

### 4) Victory Motif (Dawn Returns)
**Purpose**: Level clear, baby rescue, triumph
**Structure**: Main Xochi Motif in major mode
**Example**: C major - E - F - E - C
**Orchestration**: Add bright kalimba, ascending harp gliss, celebration shaker
**Duration**: 4-6 seconds (non-looping sting)

## III. UNIVERSAL INSTRUMENT PALETTE

**CRITICAL RULE**: Every track uses instruments from this palette ONLY. No random additions.

### Melodic Lead (choose 1 per track)
- **Quena-style flute** (breathy, earthy) → exploration, mystical moments
- **Soft synth lead** (warm saw/pulse with gentle attack) → chase, platforming energy

### Pluck/Melodic Percussion (choose 1-2 per track)
- **Marimba** (medium-low register, soft mallets) → warmth, water, night
- **Kalimba** (bright, crystalline) → dawn, victory, playful moments
- **Pizzicato strings** (or plucked synth) → chase, rhythmic drive

### Pads/Atmosphere (always present, usually 1-2 layers)
- **Warm analog pad** (gentle filter movement) → emotional core, sustain
- **Textured ambient wash** (granular, evolving) → depth, mystery
- **Choir/vocal texture** (wordless, distant) → mystical, ancient feeling

### Bass (always 1)
- **Rounded sub bass** (sine-ish, not aggressive) → foundation
- **Plucked bass** (acoustic or synth) → rhythmic sections
- **Distorted ritual drone** (boss battles only) → corruption, danger

### Percussion (layered, but always from this kit)
- **Hand drums** (djembe-style, clay pots) → core rhythm
- **Water percussion** (drips, splashes, soft strikes on liquid)
- **Shakers/seeds** (subtle, organic texture)
- **Ceremonial drums** (deeper, booming) → boss battles, dramatic moments
- **Wooden blocks/claves** (percussive accents, platforming)

### Special/Cultural Elements (use sparingly for flavor)
- **Huehuetl** (Aztec ceremonial drum) → boss intros, story beats
- **Teponaztli** (wooden slit drum) → ancient world ambience
- **Conch shell** (deep horn call) → world transitions, awakening moments

## IV. THREE CORE PALETTES (Expanded)

### PALETTE 1: Canal Nocturno (Dreamy Exploration)
**Mood**: Bioluminescent water, moonlight on reeds, gentle mystery, wonder not dread
**Tempo**: 70-95 BPM (or halftime feel at 140 with kick every 2 beats)
**Time Signature**: 4/4 or 6/8
**Key Centers**: Minor with warm extensions (Cm7, Dm9, Fm add9)
**Harmonic Movement**: i → VI → III → VII (slow drift, no rush)
**Signature Sound**: Soft marimba plucks + breathy pad + water drip percussion
**Melody Character**: Flowing, stepwise motion with occasional leaps (P4, P5)
**Bass Movement**: Whole notes or dotted half notes, minimal motion
**Layering Strategy**:
- Base: Pad + water ambience
- Layer 2: Bass enters
- Layer 3: Marimba/pluck countermelody
- Layer 4: Flute main melody (save for emotional peaks)

**Loop Structure**:
- 8 bars A (establish mood)
- 8 bars A' (add Xochi motif variation)
- 4 bars turnaround (water motif descent)
- Total: 20 bars (seamless loop)

**Use Cases**: Overworld hubs, safe zones, story cinematics, night biome exploration

---

### PALETTE 2: Chinampa Chase (Platforming Energy)
**Mood**: Playful sprinting, hopping between lily pads, hiding under leaves, urgency without panic
**Tempo**: 110-140 BPM
**Time Signature**: 6/8 (bounce feel) or swung 4/4
**Key Centers**: Minor but with brightness (Dorian mode preferred: D-E-F-G-A-B-C-D)
**Harmonic Movement**: i → IV → i → V (repetitive ostinato, hypnotic)
**Signature Sound**: Tight hand percussion + pizzicato ostinato + short flute calls
**Melody Character**: Short phrases (2-4 notes), syncopated, call-and-response
**Bass Movement**: Simple repetitive 2-bar pattern, locked with kick
**Layering Strategy**:
- Base: Percussion groove + bass
- Layer 2: Pizzicato ostinato (always on)
- Layer 3: Flute calls (add during platforming challenges)
- Layer 4: Extra percussion (shakers, blocks) for intensity peaks

**Loop Structure**:
- 4 bars intro groove (percussion + bass)
- 8 bars main section (add ostinato)
- 8 bars variation (flute calls)
- 4 bars breakdown (strip to percussion, rebuild)
- Total: 24 bars

**Use Cases**: Standard platforming levels, timed runs, chase sequences, day/dawn biomes

---

### PALETTE 3: Ajolote Mechanica (Boss/Corrupted)
**Mood**: Ancient ecology invaded by corruption, nature vs. machine, ritual fragments in distortion
**Tempo**: 90-120 BPM
**Time Signature**: 4/4 (heavy, metronomic)
**Key Centers**: Phrygian mode (E-F-G-A-B-C-D-E) for exotic tension
**Harmonic Movement**: Drone-based (pedal tone E, harmony moves above)
**Signature Sound**: Gritty analog drone + distorted bass pulse + metallic hits + ritual flute fragments
**Melody Character**: Main Xochi Motif inverted/distorted + danger motif tritones
**Bass Movement**: Aggressive syncopation, 16th note pulses
**Layering Strategy** (DKC2 Boss Phases):
- **APPROACH**: Drone + distant danger motif (minimal, building dread)
- **TELEGRAPH**: Add ceremonial drums, danger motif intensifies
- **ATTACK**: Full aggressive groove, all layers, distorted Xochi motif
- **RECOVER**: Strip to drone + single melodic fragment (player can breathe)

**Loop Structure** (4-phase adaptive):
- 8 bars APPROACH (drone + ambient tension)
- 4 bars TELEGRAPH (drums enter, danger motif)
- 16 bars ATTACK (main battle loop)
- 8 bars RECOVER (back to ambient)
- Loops: ATTACK section repeats, other phases trigger-based

**Use Cases**: Boss battles (levels 5, 10), corrupted areas, dark laboratories

## VIa. STINGER MIXING BEHAVIOR SPECIFICATION

Define how each stinger interacts with current music:

### Victory Motif (Level Clear)
- **Interrupt or Wait**: Interrupts immediately (player achievement priority)
- **Music Ducking**: Lower current music to 20% volume over 100ms
- **Post-Stinger Behavior**: After 6-second stinger completes, crossfade to next level's music OR return to hub music (fade up from 20% to 100% over 500ms)
- **Transition Logic**: If same world, crossfade to next level music; if world complete, play World Transition stinger then new world music

### Baby Rescue Stinger
- **Interrupt or Wait**: Wait for current musical phrase boundary (quantize to nearest bar)
- **Music Ducking**: Lower current music to 40% volume
- **Post-Stinger Behavior**: After 3-second stinger, fade music back up to 100% over 300ms, continue previous track
- **Special Case**: If baby rescue completes level, chain into Victory Motif (brief pause between stingers)

### Checkpoint/Healing Stinger
- **Interrupt or Wait**: Wait for phrase boundary (non-urgent)
- **Music Ducking**: Lower current music to 50% volume
- **Post-Stinger Behavior**: Fade music back to 100% over 200ms, seamlessly continue
- **Layer Note**: If checkpoint is first in level, may trigger addition of Harmony stem as reward

### Danger Alert (Enemy Proximity)
- **Interrupt or Wait**: Interrupts immediately (urgent warning)
- **Music Ducking**: Brief dip to 60% during 1.5-second sting, no fade (sharp warning)
- **Post-Stinger Behavior**: Music immediately returns to 100%, no crossfade (maintain tension)
- **Repetition Logic**: Can trigger multiple times but must have 3-second cooldown between alerts

### World Transition (Biome Change)
- **Interrupt or Wait**: Interrupts (major moment)
- **Music Ducking**: Fade out current world music completely over 1 second
- **Post-Stinger Behavior**: 8-second stinger plays in full silence, then new world music fades in over 1 second
- **Motif Bridge**: Stinger should melodically connect previous world key to new world key (dominant preparation)

### Within-World Level Transitions

**Context**: When player completes a level and moves to next level in SAME world (e.g., Dawn Level 1 → Dawn Level 2)

**Transition Behavior by Track Type:**

#### Peace/Exploration → Next Level Peace/Exploration (Same World)
- **Music continues seamlessly** (no interruption)
- Track does NOT restart; current loop position maintained
- Example: Player in DAWN_PEACE exploring Level 1, completes level, enters Level 2 still in dawn world
  - Victory stinger plays (ducks music to 20%)
  - After victory stinger, DAWN_PEACE continues from current loop position
  - No jarring restart, maintains flow

#### Chase/Platforming → Next Level Chase/Platforming (Same World)
- **Brief crossfade** (500ms)
- Restart track from beginning (platforming energy benefits from fresh start)
- Example: DAWN_CHASE Level 1 completes → Victory stinger → 500ms crossfade → DAWN_CHASE restarts for Level 2
- Rationale: Platforming tracks are high-energy; restart gives player moment to reset

#### Track Type Changes Within Same World
When transitioning between different track types in same world (e.g., Peace → Underwater → Chase):

**Peace → Underwater**:
- 1-second crossfade (gentle transition)
- Use water motif as melodic bridge if possible

**Underwater → Peace**:
- 1-second crossfade
- Surface sounds (bubbles rising) can mask transition

**Peace/Underwater → Chase**:
- 750ms crossfade (slightly faster for energy shift)
- Brief stinger optional: 2-note "let's go" cue (danger motif rhythm without full melody)

**Chase → Peace/Underwater**:
- 1.5-second crossfade (longer to calm down from intense energy)
- Fade out percussion stem first (500ms), then crossfade remaining stems

**Any → Upscroller**:
- Immediate crossfade (300ms) - urgency required
- Upscroller music interrupts as water rising is sudden threat

**Upscroller → Any**:
- 1-second crossfade (relief from intensity)
- Optional: brief "safe" stinger (2 notes from checkpoint theme)

#### Special Case: Menu/Hub Transitions
- Entering hub from any level: 2-second crossfade (decompression time)
- Exiting hub to level: 1-second crossfade (building energy)
- Hub music always restarts from beginning (consistent welcoming feeling)

#### Implementation Logic:
```pseudocode
function onLevelComplete(currentWorld, currentLevel, nextLevel) {
  // Play victory stinger first
  playStinger("VICTORY") // This ducks current music to 20%

  if (nextLevel.world != currentWorld) {
    // World change: use World Transition stinger
    playStinger("WORLD_TRANSITION")
    music.fadeIn(getWorldMusic(nextLevel.world), duration: 1000ms)
  } else {
    // Same world transition
    currentTrackType = getCurrentTrackType() // e.g., "PEACE", "CHASE", etc.
    nextTrackType = getTrackTypeForLevel(nextLevel)

    if (currentTrackType == nextTrackType) {
      // Same track type within world
      if (currentTrackType == "PEACE" || currentTrackType == "UNDERWATER" || currentTrackType == "MENU") {
        // Continue seamlessly (music already playing, just un-duck from victory stinger)
        music.fadeVolume(current, target: 1.0, duration: 500ms)
      } else if (currentTrackType == "CHASE" || currentTrackType == "UPSCROLLER") {
        // Restart for fresh energy
        music.crossfade(getWorldTrack(currentWorld, currentTrackType), duration: 500ms, fromStart: true)
      }
    } else {
      // Different track types, need crossfade
      crossfadeDuration = calculateCrossfadeDuration(currentTrackType, nextTrackType)
      music.crossfade(getWorldTrack(currentWorld, nextTrackType), duration: crossfadeDuration)
    }
  }
}

function calculateCrossfadeDuration(fromType, toType) {
  if (toType == "UPSCROLLER") return 300ms // Urgent
  if (fromType == "CHASE" && (toType == "PEACE" || toType == "UNDERWATER")) return 1500ms // Calming
  if (fromType == "PEACE" && toType == "CHASE") return 750ms // Building energy
  if (toType == "MENU" || fromType == "MENU") return 2000ms // Hub buffer
  return 1000ms // Default gentle crossfade
}
```

#### Player Experience Goal:
- **Same world = musical cohesion**: Player feels they're in one continuous space
- **World change = significant shift**: Big moment, celebrated with transition stinger
- **Energy shifts are gradual**: No whiplash from peaceful to intense (except upscroller danger)
- **Victory doesn't reset progress**: Music continues flowing, victory is an accent not a stop

- **Music Ducking**: Abrupt cut of all music (silence)
- **Post-Stinger Behavior**: 3-second sting plays in silence. Upon respawn, previous music resumes from beginning with 1-second fade-in

## VII. STEM EXPORT & GAME ENGINE INTEGRATION

### Stem Architecture (for all looping tracks)
Export each track as 4 separate stems for dynamic mixing:

1. **BASE STEM** (always playing):
   - Pad layers
   - Ambient textures
   - Water/environmental sounds

2. **PERCUSSION STEM** (adds intensity):
   - All hand drums
   - Shakers
   - Wooden blocks/claves
   - Trigger: Platforming challenges, enemy proximity, upscroller sections

3. **HARMONY STEM** (adds emotional depth):
   - Bass
   - Marimba/kalimba harmonic layers (not lead melody)
   - Chord pads
   - Trigger: Story moments, safe zones, emotional peaks

4. **MELODY STEM** (focal point):
   - Flute lead
   - Marimba/kalimba melodic lead
   - Featured motif statements
   - Trigger: Key gameplay moments, victories, player achievements

### CRITICAL: Stem Extraction Reality

**Suno and Udio do NOT natively export stems.** You will receive only a stereo mix from AI generation tools.

#### Recommended Stem Separation Tools:
1. **iZotope RX 10** (Music Rebalance module) - Professional grade, best quality
2. **Moises.ai** - Web-based, good quality, fast turnaround
3. **LALAL.AI** - Reliable, specialized in instrument separation
4. **SpectraLayers Pro 10** (Steinberg) - Advanced spectral editing, manual cleanup

#### Stem Extraction Workflow:
```
1. GENERATE: Create full stereo mix via Suno/Udio
2. EXPORT: Download highest quality WAV (not MP3)
3. SEPARATE:
   - Upload to stem separation tool
   - Select 4-stem output: Percussion, Bass/Harmony, Melody, Ambience
   - Process (typically 2-5 minutes per track)
4. CLEANUP (Manual DAW work):
   - Import all 4 stems into DAW
   - Listen for cross-bleed (e.g., percussion in melody stem)
   - Use EQ to reduce bleed (high-pass melody to remove bass bleed, etc.)
   - Use spectral editing tools to surgically remove artifacts
   - Re-balance stem volumes to match original mix
5. QUALITY CHECK:
   - Solo each stem: Does it contain ONLY its intended elements?
   - Measure cross-bleed: Use spectrum analyzer, aim for <5% energy from other stems
   - Sum all stems: Does it match original mix? (Should be 95%+ identical)
6. RENDER: Export each stem as 48kHz/24-bit WAV with identical length and loop points
```

#### Quality Criteria:
- **Acceptable cross-bleed**: <5% spectral energy from other stems
- **Artifact tolerance**: Minor digital artifacts acceptable if not audible in game mix
- **Phase coherence**: All stems must be perfectly time-aligned (zero latency offset)

#### Time Estimates:
- **Per track**: 2-3 hours (generation + separation + cleanup)
- **30 looping tracks**: 60-90 hours total
- **Boss tracks** (multi-phase): Add 1-2 hours per boss for phase separation
- **Stingers**: Minimal cleanup needed (mostly mono or simple stereo)

#### Budget Implications:
- **iZotope RX**: $399 one-time (recommended if doing 30+ tracks)
- **Moises.ai**: ~$12/month (20 hours processing, sufficient for this project)
- **LALAL.AI**: ~$25 for 500 minutes (covers ~15-20 tracks)
- **Recommended**: Moises.ai subscription for bulk work + iZotope RX trial for critical boss tracks

#### Fallback Strategy:
If stem separation quality is unacceptable for a track:
1. Regenerate with simpler instrumentation (fewer layers = cleaner separation)
2. Commission hybrid approach: AI generates melodic idea, human performs stems separately
3. Budget 15% of tracks may need human re-recording for stem purity

   - Clean loop transition points

   - Trim to exact bar counts
   - Add crossfades for seamless looping
   - Isolate stems if AI provides separation
   - EQ adjustments for game audio mix

**Reality Check**: AI music generation has ~30-50% usable output rate. Plan for failures.

- Vocals appear despite "no vocals, instrumental only" prompt

5. Iterate based on feedback

#### Fatigue Testing Protocol
Execute this protocol for EVERY looping track before approval:

**Test Setup:**
- 3-5 playtesters (mix of team members and external)
- Headphones (closed-back, to simulate focused listening)
- Track loops continuously for 10 minutes
- Playtesters perform representative gameplay (not idle listening)

**Rating Schedule:**
Playtesters rate annoyance on 1-5 scale every 2 minutes:
- **Minute 0**: Baseline (how do you feel about this track?)
- **Minute 2**: First loop fatigue check
- **Minute 4**: Pattern recognition fatigue
- **Minute 6**: Melody saturation check
- **Minute 8**: Critical endurance point
- **Minute 10**: Final verdict

**Rating Scale:**
1. Love it, could listen for hours
2. Pleasant, no issues
3. Starting to notice repetition, mild annoyance
4. Repetitive, wanting it to change
5. Actively annoying, need it to stop

**Pass Criteria:**
- **No individual rating above 3** at any time point
- **Average across all testers and time points ≤ 2.0**
- **Minute 10 average ≤ 2.5** (endurance test)

**Failure Actions (if criteria not met):**

If average rating 2.5-3.0:
1. **Extend loop length**: Add 4-8 bars of variation before repeat
2. **Add subtle variation**: Alternate percussion pattern every other loop
3. **EQ adjustment**: Reduce harsh frequencies (2-4kHz, 6-8kHz sibilance)

If average rating 3.0-4.0:
1. **Melody simplification**: Remove or space out repetitive melodic phrases
2. **Dynamic variation**: Create A/B loop variants that alternate
3. **Frequency masking**: Lower volume of most repetitive element by 3-6dB

If average rating >4.0:
1. **Regenerate track**: Current version fundamentally fatiguing
2. **Slower tempo**: Try -10 BPM
3. **Sparser instrumentation**: Remove one melodic layer entirely


**Special Considerations:**
- **Upscroller tracks**: Higher tolerance (rating 3.0 acceptable) due to short gameplay duration
- **Menu/Hub tracks**: Strictest testing (must average ≤1.5) as players may idle for 10+ minutes

## XIII. FINAL NOTES

### Flexibility & Iteration
This specification is comprehensive but not rigid. During implementation:
- **Trust your ears**: If a prompt produces something magical that breaks a "rule" but serves the game, use it
- **Iterate motifs**: If Xochi motif isn't landing after testing, evolve it (but maintain consistency once locked)
- **Player feedback**: Fatigue testing with real players is critical (what sounds good for 30 seconds may annoy after 10 minutes)
- **Adjust BPMs**: If platforming feels sluggish at 120 BPM, try 130 (but keep world consistency)

### AI Generation Realities
- **Expect 30-50% hit rate**: Not every AI generation will be usable; generate in batches
- **Hybrid approach**: Use AI for base loops, then edit in DAW for perfection (trim, add crossfades, adjust levels)
- **Stem extraction**: If AI doesn't provide stems, use tools like iZotope RX, Moises.ai, or LALAL.AI for post-separation
- **Reference tracks**: Generate your own reference pack from prompts, then refine vocabulary for better results

### Cultural Consultation
- **Consider hiring a consultant**: Even with research, having someone from Xochimilco or with deep Mexican musical knowledge review the score adds authenticity
- **Language check**: If adding any Spanish lyrics/words (not recommended for instrumental score, but for titles), verify correctness
- **Community feedback**: Share work-in-progress with Mexican game dev communities for respectful representation

### The Core Goal
Create a soundtrack that:
1. **Players whistle after playing** (memorable melodic identity)
2. **Feels like Xochimilco** (specific place, not generic Mexico)
3. **Enhances gameplay** (supports platforming, doesn't distract)
4. **Honors culture** (respectful, researched, not stereotypical)
5. **Loops forever** (seamless, fatigue-resistant)
